# F2.7 — Multi-select region operations

**Seed:** `tests/e2e/seed.spec.ts`
**Roadmap status:** ✅ done (`01d6b46`)

## Application Overview

The AI-edition editor renders **regions** (zoom, annotation, speed) on lanes below the clip track. Users need to operate on multiple regions at once: select several, then delete them with a single keystroke, or move them together. The roadmap says **shift-click** adds a region to the multi-selection; **Delete** removes the entire multi-selection.

## Test Scenarios

### 1. Shift-click adds a region to the selection

**Setup**

1. Open the editor at `http://localhost:5173/?windowType=editor`.
2. Apply a project with at least three regions of any kind on the same lane (or across lanes — both should work).
3. Identify three distinct region pills (e.g. via `data-testid="region-pill"` or by their text label / position).

**Steps**

1. Click the first region pill.
2. **Shift-click** the second region pill.
3. **Shift-click** the third region pill.

**Expected results**

- After step 1, only the first pill shows a "selected" visual state.
- After step 2, the first and second pills show selected; the third does not.
- After step 3, all three pills show selected.
- The `RightPanelStack` region inspector (right sidebar) reflects the multi-selection: it should show a multi-select header ("3 selected" or similar) or at least not show single-region details.

### 2. Delete removes the entire multi-selection

**Setup**

1. Same project as scenario 1, with three regions pre-selected.

**Steps**

1. Press `Delete` (or `Backspace`).

**Expected results**

- All three selected regions are removed from the timeline.
- The lane's `data-annotation-count`, `data-zoom-range-count`, etc. attributes on the `data-testid="timeline-pane"` element each drop by the count of removed regions.
- No console errors fire during the operation.

### 3. Clicking a selected region without shift removes it from the selection

**Setup**

1. Same project, two regions pre-selected.

**Steps**

1. Click one of the two selected regions **without** holding shift.

**Expected results**

- Only the clicked region remains selected; the other is deselected.
- The inspector switches back to single-region mode.
