# P3.7 — Timeline ruler hover scrub feedback

**Seed:** `tests/e2e/seed.spec.ts`
**Roadmap status:** ✅ done (`76d78db`)

## Application Overview

When the user hovers the mouse over the timeline ruler, the editor shows a thin **vertical hover-guide line** at the cursor's x position plus a small **time chip** showing the source time under the cursor. This is purely visual feedback — the playhead does not move until the user clicks. The guide and chip disappear when the pointer leaves the ruler.

## Test Scenarios

### 1. Hovering the ruler shows a hover-guide line and time chip

**Setup**

1. Open the editor at `http://localhost:5173/?windowType=editor`.
2. Apply a project with at least one clip.

**Steps**

1. Move the mouse over the ruler area (top 22px of the timeline viewport).
2. Move the mouse to roughly the **middle** of the ruler.
3. Read the visible elements in the ruler.
4. Move the mouse off the timeline (e.g. to the empty area below the bottombar).
5. Read the visible elements again.

**Expected results**

- After step 2, a thin vertical line (`hoverGuide` class, 1px wide, accent color) is rendered at the cursor's x.
- A small chip (`dragTooltip` class) appears next to the line showing the time under the cursor in `M:SS.s` format.
- After step 4, the hover-guide line and the chip are no longer visible.
- No console errors fire during hover.
