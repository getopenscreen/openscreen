# Quick start

This walks through recording, trimming, and exporting your first video. See [Installation](./installation.md) first if you haven't installed OpenScreen yet.

## 1. Open the recording HUD

Launching OpenScreen shows a small floating pill (the HUD) docked at the bottom of your screen. It stays on top of everything and doesn't intercept clicks until you interact with it.

## 2. Pick what to record

Click the source picker (screen icon) to open the source selector. It lists your **Screens** and **Windows** in two tabs — pick a thumbnail and hit **Share**.

## 3. Turn on audio and webcam (optional)

In the HUD's audio group, toggle:
- **System audio** — captures what's playing on your machine.
- **Microphone** — opens a level meter and a device picker so you can confirm the right mic is selected.
- **Webcam** — opens a camera picker; the webcam records as a separate track you'll position later in the editor.

## 4. Record

Click the record button. A 3‑2‑1 countdown appears over your desktop, then recording starts. While recording you can:
- **Pause / Resume**
- **Restart** — discard the current take and start over
- **Cancel** — discard without saving

Click **Stop** when you're done.

## 5. Open the Studio

Click **Open Studio** (or it opens automatically after stopping) to load your recording into the editor.

## 6. Trim and export

- Click the scissors **Trim** button (or press `T`) to arm the cut tool, then click anywhere on the timeline to drop a cut — drag its edges to adjust what gets removed.
- Click **Export** in the top bar, choose **MP4** or **GIF**, pick a quality, and hit **Export**.
- When it finishes, click **Show in folder** to find your file.

That's the core loop. For the full editing toolset — zooms, speed changes, captions, cursor styling, webcam layout — see [Editing & timeline](./editing-timeline.md).

:::tip
Save your work as a project (`⌘/Ctrl S`) before exporting if you want to come back and keep editing later — `.openscreen` project files keep every layer editable, unlike the exported video.
:::
